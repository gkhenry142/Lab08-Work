City of Wichita

  • Wichita is the largest city in Kansas, with a population of about 650,000 people.
  • It began as a merchant center along the Arkansas River, but evolved into a major center for building aircraft.
  • In fact, the city is known as the "Air Capital of the World," home to Spirit Aerosystems, Textron Aviation, and Hawker Beechcraft.
  • In pop culture, the sheriff Wyatt Earp featured in the movie "Tombstone" is a character who was a former law enforcement officer in Wichita

Wichita is my hometown. Until leaving to study at the University of Kansas, I lived my whole life in Wichita.
It may not have as many cool things to do as in Kansas City, but I still think it is a great place to raise a family.
Although the town is bustling with people, the street and highway traffic never gets as bad as it does in Kansas City.
Also, my home is only 5-10 minutes away from the grocery store, the movie theater, the gym, the library, and my high school.
The cost of living is also reasonable, which makes it a great city to start a career and accumulate wealth for retirement.
